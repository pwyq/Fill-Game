Rules - Fill-Game


Rules of Fillomino and Fill-game: Fill-game is an adversarial two player version of the popular puzzle called Fillomino. In Fillomino, the player tries to fill a grid with numbers while satisfying constraints on these numbers. Roughly speaking, each region of same-numbered squares must have as many squares as the label on those squares indicates. The pictures below, from Wikipedia, show a puzzle and its solution.
To understand this puzzle in detail, please refer to the wikipedia Fillomino page.

Fill-game is an adversarial two player game loosely based on Fillomino. The goal is to make the last move, or equivalently, to set up a position where the opponent has no legal move left on their turn.

The differences in the rules to make it a meaningful two player game are:

  1. The only numbers allowed are 1, 2, 3 and 4.
  2. The constraint on the moves is that a number that you place may not immediately violate the Fillomino rules. For example, you cannot play a 1 next to another 1, or a 2 next to two other 2, etc. You also cannot create a region that is fully surrounded and has the wrong number on it, such as a fully surrounded region of only two '3'. Also see the example below.
  3. It is OK to play a move that will make the puzzle impossible to solve in the future. In fact, doing this well is the main strategy for winning the game.
